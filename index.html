About Us

Background and Certifications

IFOC Chaplains are among some of the most anointed and dedicated men and women in the field of Chaplaincy. We know that the fields are ripe unto the harvest and the workers are few. Gray Stallworth was certified as an Ordained Chaplain and is a designated Senior Chaplain in the International Fellowship of Chaplains, Inc. Active in the United Methodist Church, he is a Certified Lay Servant Leader. He serves on the Church Council as a member-at-large and has previously served on multiple committees at churches where he has worshipped and belonged.

Why Spiritual Fitness is Important

Each side of the YMCA’s triangle is of equal length, thus it is known as an equilateral triangle. This suggests that all three sides - representing spirit, mind, and body - deserve equal attention and development. I recognized the triangle long before I began my YMCA professional career. And obviously, it’s been in front of me for all those years! While many YMCAs have allowed somewhat of a “mission drift” many associations do a wonderful job of keeping the “C” strong in the YMCA. You can find many examples of how they do this, but you’d have to look pretty closely. Even beyond the YMCA, many would agree that as individuals we do need to maintain a healthy balance between our physical, mental and spiritual health. Thus, maintaining our spiritual fitness is an important part of our well-being. Being “fit” in any of these areas is not easy. You can’t just drink a special solution, take a certain pill, or even read a self-improvement book. It takes work. I’ve chosen to focus on the “spiritual” so that individuals can maintain an often neglected side of the triangle.

Chaplian Services

A ministry of presence- A chaplain can serve your agency, organization or business. From regular visits to the workplace, devotions at employee/staff meetings or functions to visitation and pastorla counseling, Spiritual Fitness Ministries can work with you to establish a program to fit your needs. Demonstrate how you feel about the total well being of your workforce by adding a chaplian.

Prayer Ministry- George Williams, the founder of the first Young Men's Christian Association, believed strogly in prayer. He and the other men, at the founding of the YMCA, made a practice of praying specifically for people by name. That practice continues and we maintain serveral 'prayer list' where individual names are lifted up in prayer. For instance, rahter than praying for all the schools as the new school year approaches, we'd rather pray for a list of individula teachers by name. It would take some time, but our Lord wants a personal relationship with each of us, so why shouldn't we make our prayers specifacally for persons by name. Spiritual Fitness Ministries can organize your supporters into a prayer team designed to champion your ministry by intercessory prayer.

This is an appeal to my YMCA friends and colleauges all over the country. With fewer confrences being held where the Ragger program is introduced and participants are givent the opportunity to take part, I am often asked what will happen to the program. I firmly beleive that God will both perserve and promote this program and that His plan for the program will play out just like He wants it.

I also beleive that the resident camps across the YMCA movment will continue to be the 'home' of the Ragger Society. The program is simply designed and perfcet for the resident camp where campers participate over at least a one week period. The program is still relevant today! Many camps still conduct the program, but the challenge remains to find someone hired at camp to be the champion for the program and make sure young staff are exposed to it and have the opportunity to particapte. Those staff who will return to work for several summers can 'carry' the program on to a new wave of Raggers. Let's keep this going!

That is not to say that summer day camp programs aren't a good fit. I have always thought the day camp staff should have the opportunity to learn about the Raggers and participate over teh summer. For some, an intruduction during training week is a great way to do that. perhaps day camp staff training also includes ceremonies for any staff who have accepted a challenge. There could also be other opportunitites during the summer season.

Many camps will conduct the program, create a Raggers Point, but after a season or two, find themselves without someone to get it rekindled! There are Raggers who will gladly assist you in getting the traditional Raggers Program back into your camp.

As some of you know, the Raggers Creed is from a hymn, written in the early 1900’s. Listen to the first two verses of the hymn here! I hope you enjoy it!

If you would like ANY assistance at all with your Raggers Program, no matter if its to get started for the first time or to rekindle it, please feel free to contact me about available resources.
